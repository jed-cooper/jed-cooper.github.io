DATA ANALYSIS
PROJECT 7

SUMMARY

This data project uses web scraping and sentiment analysis to explore online news articles from the nuclear and renewable energy sectors, leveraging data to gain insights into investment opportunities nd stock performance.

Key Insights:
1. Sentiment surrounding renewable energy is very positive, with values for articles remaining consistently high (sentiment > 0.98).
2. Sentiment surrounding nuclear energy is also very positive, with values for articles remaining consistently high (sentiment > 0.97).

CHALLENGES

ethical and legal impact of web scraping:
- scraping data can provide highly valuable insights for any person or organisation capable of leveraging that data.
- relevant laws & regulations apply, depending upon the country where scraping is conducted.
- this analysis was conducted within the UK, so applicable laws are found within the Computer Misuse Act 1990, the Data Protection Act 2018, and the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988.

TECHNIQUES

scrapy:
- used a spider class to parse news articles via HTML/CSS tags.
- used the response/get functions to extract data.
- executed spider via command line and stored data in json/csv files.

natural language toolkit:
- used tokenisation to split text into identifiable words.
- used word sentiment analysis to identify positive/negative words & their frequency.
- used stemming & lemmatization to reduce words to their root forms.

EXTENSIONS

The project could be extended to include:
- sentiment scores for the fossil fuel sector.
- correlations between sentiment and stock price for each sector over a given period.
- a live display including sentiment scores & stock prices for all sectors, which automatically updates every day using news articles & stock price data.
